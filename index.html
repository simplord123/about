“A Reflection of the Fallen Blade”

The end of (Edo Period 1868)



I have fallen
more times than I can count.
Each time,
I shattered —
not just bone or blade,
but the man I thought I was.


From blood-stained soil
I gathered the fragments
of who I used to be,
cutting my hands
on memory,
on failure,
on the echo of a name once proud.


They thought I was beaten.
Even I believed it once.
But fire does not only destroy —
it forges.


From ash, I rise.
Again.
And again.
And again.


Not for vengeance.
Not for glory.
But for the promise
that the battle inside me
can be won.


I faced the enemy
with my own eyes.
He wore my doubts.
He spoke in my voice.
He knew my every weakness.


But I stood.
Not as the man I was —
but as the warrior I became.
Each scar, a lesson.
Each fall, a trial.
Each breath, defiance.


And in the silence after the final clash,
when my blade no longer trembled,
I found something greater than victory:
peace.


Not handed to me —
but earned.
Carved from pain.
Tempered in flame.
Paid for in full.


I walk now not to escape the past,
but to honor it.
To carry the weight,
without letting it break me.


This is my path.
Not of perfection —
but of perseverance.


From ash, I rise.
And from within,
I am whole.
---


Author: Yvan Pogi
