“Stone, Blood, and Light”


Stone cuts through my palms —
still, I climb with silent breath.
Wind sings of the pain.


Burdened by the weight,
I kneel so others may rise —
honor tastes like blood.


Above, light breaks through.
I reach back with trembling hands —
glory is not mine.
